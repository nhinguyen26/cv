Cardiovascular Classifier Project

As a Project Developer for DataGood, an organization dedicated to data science projects for social good, I have developed a machine learning classifier to predict various severities of cardiovascular disease and heart failures in patients. I have performed exploratory data analysis and visualizations to examine the trends in health data, tested machine learning algorithms such as linear, logistic regression, random forests, gradient boosting, and applied principal component analysis on heart disease and failure datasets. The final product is a graphical user interface that takes in patient data and output a prediction on their health condition.

The Materials Project

As a Data Analyst and Researcher for the Alivisatos Undergraduate Research Program, I have completed a research project on the computational investigation of metal oxides for antimicrobial and anti-UV textile applications. In this project, I have extracted, visualized, analyzed data on metal oxdies from the Materials Project Database with Python Materials Genomics to determine the desired chemical properties and extrapolated ideal compounds for real-world applications such as use in hospital or for sunblocking.
